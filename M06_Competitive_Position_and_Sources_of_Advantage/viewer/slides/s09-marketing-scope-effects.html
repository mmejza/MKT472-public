Marketing Cost Scope Effects
Spreading marketing expense across more products lowers cost per unit — structural advantage over single-brand rivals
Salesforce Scope — P&G
Problem
Each detergent brand needs sales coverage of retail accounts
Scope
One salesperson sells Tide + Cheer + Dash + Gain in a single retail call
Effect
Sales expense per pound of detergent falls as brands increase
Competitor
Single-brand rival: same call frequency, higher cost per unit sold
Advertising Scope — Campbell's
Problem
Each soup flavor needs advertising to maintain awareness
Scope
Campbell's ad reinforces brand + adjacent flavors simultaneously
Effect
Ad spend per ounce of soup falls as flavor count increases
Competitor
Fewer flavors = higher ad cost per unit sold
