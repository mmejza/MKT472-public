What Makes an Advantage Defensible?
"An effective competitive advantage should be substantial, sustainable, and leveraged." — David Aaker
SUBSTANTIAL
Product or service noticeably superior — supports higher price OR sufficient value at a price competitors cannot match.
e.g., Louis Vuitton commands a premium. Walmart commands the lowest price. Both are substantial.
SUSTAINABLE
Difficult for competitors to quickly imitate — protected by patents, processes, relationships, or embedded knowledge.
e.g., Humira patent protects AbbVie's advantage for years. Dell's operations model persists as long as rivals cannot replicate it.
LEVERAGED
Advantage extends to other product lines in the firm's portfolio — multiplies return on core capability.
e.g., Honda's engine technology advantage transfers across automobiles, motorcycles, lawnmowers, and generators.
