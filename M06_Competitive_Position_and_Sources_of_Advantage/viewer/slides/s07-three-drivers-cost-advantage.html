Three Drivers of Variable Cost Advantage
Scale, Scope, and Learning — distinct mechanisms, can coexist
How
Mechanism
Example
Scale Effect
Unit cost falls as output volume increases
Fixed costs spread over more units; purchasing economies at higher volume
Cellular service: cost per subscriber falls 20% each time total subscribers double
Scope Effect
Shared inputs across product lines lower per-unit cost
Same component, process, or sales call serves multiple products simultaneously
Honda ignition switch used across cars, motorcycles, lawnmowers, generators
Learning Effect
Cost falls as cumulative production experience grows
Process improvements from learning reduce labor and waste per unit over time
Each unit produced reveals opportunities to build the next unit more efficiently
When All Three Coexist

A firm with high volume, broad product lines, AND production experience achieves the deepest cost advantage — each effect reinforces the others.
