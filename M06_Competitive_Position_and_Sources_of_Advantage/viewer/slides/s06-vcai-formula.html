Variable Cost Advantage Index
VCAI measures relative cost efficiency — index below 100 signals cost advantage
VCAI = (Firm COGS %) ÷ (Avg. Competitor COGS %) × 100
Components
COGS % — Cost of goods sold as % of sales (variable cost per dollar of revenue)
Avg. Competitor — Simple average of top 3 competitors' COGS percentages
Direction Rule
Index < 100 → cost advantage (firm is leaner)
Index ≈ 100 → parity
Index > 100 → cost disadvantage
Two Things to Notice

1 — The denominator is the average of the top 3 — not just the lowest-cost competitor. A firm can hold a cost advantage over the average while one competitor still undercuts it.

2 — A VCAI of 80 means the firm's COGS is 80% of the competitor average — 20 cents cheaper per dollar of revenue. That margin difference compounds across millions of units.
