Porter's Five Forces: Key Determinants
Conditions that make each force high or low — needed to score IAI accurately
Entry Barriers — High When:
· Economies of scale in production or marketing
· Strong brand identity among incumbents
· High capital requirements for entry
· Proprietary technology or patents
Substitutes — Many When:
· Relative price of substitutes is low
· Low switching costs between alternatives
· High functional similarity to substitutes
· Strong buyer propensity to substitute
Buyer Power — High When:
· Buyer concentration (few large buyers)
· Low switching costs between suppliers
· Product not important to buyer's operations
· Buyers can backward-integrate
Supplier Power — High When:
· Supplier concentration (few suppliers)
· Differentiated inputs with high switching costs
· Supplier can forward-integrate
· Buyer has few alternative sources
