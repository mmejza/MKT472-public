RMS: Worked Example
Google Search (2010) — RMS = 199: holds more share than top 3 combined
Search Engine Market Share Role RMS
Google 65.5% Firm 65.5 ÷ (15.9+14.1+2.9) × 100 = 199
Yahoo 15.9% Competitor 1 19
Bing 14.1% Competitor 2 17
Ask 2.9% Competitor 3 —
RMS = 199 → DOMINANT LEADER
Google holds nearly twice the share of its top 3 competitors combined. RMS above 100 is the definition of market dominance.
Why RMS > 100 Matters Strategically

Market dominance via RMS unlocks network effects, scale economies, and advertising efficiency that followers cannot access. RMS leadership is a self-reinforcing advantage — it feeds the cost and brand advantages covered in LO2.
