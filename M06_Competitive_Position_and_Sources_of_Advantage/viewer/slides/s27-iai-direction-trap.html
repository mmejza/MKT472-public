The IAI Direction Trap
Exit barriers — the most commonly mis-scored Porter force
Common Error
"High exit barriers make it easier for weak competitors to leave, so the industry is more attractive."
Why It Seems Correct
"High barriers" sounds like it would keep out competition and protect market position — analogous to entry barriers that block new entrants.
Students import the logic of entry barriers and apply it incorrectly to exit barriers.
Why It Is Wrong
HIGH exit barriers trap weak, unprofitable competitors inside the market — they cannot leave even when losing money. This intensifies rivalry and destroys margins for everyone, including profitable firms.
Correct Rule
LOW exit barriers → attractive. Weak competitors can exit quickly, leaving the market to stronger players with better margins and less destructive competition.
Direction Summary — Memorize These
↑ Higher Entry Barriers  ·  ↓ Lower Exit Barriers  ·  ↓ Lower Buyer Power
↓ Lower Supplier Power  ·  ↓ Fewer Substitutes  ·  ↓ Lower Rivalry  = ATTRACTIVE
