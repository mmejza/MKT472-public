Cost of Purchase Index: The Sign Trap
A high RA score on a cost attribute means you charge MORE — not that you are better on price
✗ Wrong Conclusion
"Our Purchase Price RA score is +27 — we are better than competitors on price."

This reverses the sign logic. A positive RA on a cost attribute means the customer rates your cost as higher than competitors.

Higher perceived cost → worse competitive position on price → cost disadvantage.
✓ Correct Logic
For cost components, the rating scale runs in the OPPOSITE direction:

High cost RA score = you charge MORE = worse competitive position
Low / negative cost RA score = you charge LESS = better competitive position

Formula is the same: Cost of Purchase Index = 100 + (sum of cost RA scores).
Index >100 = more expensive than competitive average.
The Full PCV Logic

PCV Index = Overall Performance Index − Cost of Purchase Index. If Cost Index >100 (you charge more), it subtracts from perceived value. A firm with Performance Index 137 but Cost Index 110 creates less perceived value than one with Performance 120 and Cost 95.
