Economic Value: Formula and Logic
Value is measured from the buyer's perspective — total lifecycle cost, not purchase price
Economic Value Formula
Economic Value = Competitor LCC − Company LCC
LCC = Total Lifecycle Cost = sum of all cost components over the product's full life
1
A product with a higher purchase price can still create positive economic value — if its other lifecycle costs are low enough to produce a lower total LCC.
2
Economic value is measured from the buyer's perspective: how much does the buyer save over the product's full life compared to the competitor's product?
Weyerhaeuser — Gate 1

Particleboard cost furniture makers MORE to buy — but LESS to use. It eliminated lamination steps and blade-sharpening costs entirely. Total lifecycle cost was lower. A price-focused analysis would have rejected the product. pp. 118–119.
