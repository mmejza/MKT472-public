Emotional Value: Brand Personality and Customer Value
When economic and performance value converge, brand personality becomes the primary differentiator
Emotional Value Chain
Psychological Need
→
Brand Personality
→
Emotional Benefit
→
Customer Value
Example: Customer with a need to be seen as rugged and self-sufficient is drawn to a Chevy pickup advertised as durable and tough. The Ruggedness brand personality delivers an emotional benefit that contributes to overall customer value — independent of the truck's objective performance specifications.
1
Emotional value occurs when a brand's meaning resonates with the psychological needs, personality, or personal values of buyers in a target market — not just rational preferences.
2
Brands that match customer psychological needs command loyalty that price alone cannot explain — a competitor matching on economic and performance value may still lose if emotional fit is absent.
Gate 1 — Five Types: Ch. 4, p. 143
Sincerity · Excitement · Competence · Sophistication · Ruggedness — each with defining traits. Gate 1 presents novel scenarios; students must identify type from description, not memorized examples.
