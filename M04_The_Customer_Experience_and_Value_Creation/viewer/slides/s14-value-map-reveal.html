Value Map: Superior vs. Inferior Value Zones
The fair value line divides the market — actual price vs. fair price determines zone
Superior Value
Actual price below fair price
Customer receives more than the market says it is worth
CV > 0
Fair Value Line
Actual price equals fair price
Customer receives exactly what the market says it is worth
CV = 0
Inferior Value
Actual price above fair price
Customer pays more than the market says it is worth
CV < 0
Canon A590 & Fuji J10 — Superior Value
Canon A590: Rating 80, Price $180 vs. Fair $224 → CV = +$44
Fuji J10: Rating 70, Price $130 vs. Fair $202 → CV = +$72
Both priced well below their fair value — strong customer value propositions.
HP PhotoSmart Premium — Underpriced
High performance (rating 120), low actual price ($140) — fair price substantially higher.
Result: Superior value delivered to customers, but margin surrendered by HP.
A high-performing product priced below fair price creates value but leaves money on the table.
Managerial Insight

The value map reveals both underpriced products (firm sacrifices margin) and overpriced products (firm risks customer defection). Price parity does not mean value parity — same price at different performance levels produces different customer value.
