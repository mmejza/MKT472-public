Total Customer Experience — Three-Stage Model
Purchase, Usage, Replacement — product-focused firms neglect usage and replacement, where 60–80% of lifecycle costs occur. Fig 4-2, pp. 116–117.
2
Lifecycle Cost Categories — Six Types with Named Examples
One company example per category: Sealed Air AirCap, Loctite Quick Metal, Kathon, American Hospital Supply, GE Capital. pp. 126–132, Figs 4-11 to 4-15.
3
Weyerhaeuser Particleboard Case
Higher purchase price, lower total lifecycle cost — eliminated customer lamination and blade-sharpening costs. Economic value discovered through customer observation, not assumption. pp. 118–119, 127.
4
The 8% / 80% Perception Gap
Only 8% of customers rate their experience as superior — yet 80% of companies believe they deliver a superior experience. Gap exists because companies rely on internal measurement. Ch. 4 opening, p. 115.
5
Brand Personality — Five Types with Defining Traits
Sincerity · Excitement · Competence · Sophistication · Ruggedness. Gate 1 presents novel brand scenarios — match to type using traits, not named examples. Fig 4-27, pp. 143–144.
