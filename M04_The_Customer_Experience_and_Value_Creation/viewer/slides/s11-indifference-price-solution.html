Indifference Price: Solution
The indifference price is the pricing ceiling — charging above it destroys value for the buyer
Indifference Price Formula
Indifference Price = Competitor LCC − Company Ownership Costs
= $1,700 − $400 = $1,300
What the Ceiling Means
Below $1,300: Buyer gains value — LCC < competitor
At $1,300: Buyer is exactly indifferent — LCC = $1,700
Above $1,300: Buyer loses value — LCC > competitor
ScenarioPriceTotal LCCEconomic Value Created

Price 3 $1,150 $1,550 +$150 (buyer gains)
Pricing Ceiling Rule

The indifference price is the maximum price the firm can charge while still delivering non-negative economic value. Charging above it is not just a competitive risk — it actively destroys the buyer's economic position.
