Six Lifecycle Cost Categories
Purchase price is the most visible cost — rarely the largest over the product's full life
Price Paid
Direct purchase price — the most visible cost, but often not the largest component of total lifecycle cost
Acquisition Costs
Ordering, logistics, receiving, and inventory management costs incurred before use begins
Usage Costs
Labor and operating costs incurred while the product is in use — often the largest lifecycle cost category
Financing & Insurance
Interest charges and insurance premiums on high-value capital assets
Maintenance Costs
Repair, scheduled servicing, and downtime costs — reliability differences across products show up here
Disposal Costs
Waste removal, environmental compliance, and recycling costs at end of product life
