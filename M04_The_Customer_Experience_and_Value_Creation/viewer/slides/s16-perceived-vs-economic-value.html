Perceived Value vs. Economic and Performance Value
The measurement method differs — perceived value cannot be calculated without customer input
Economic & Performance Value
Derived from objective data — prices, lifecycle costs, performance ratings

Company can calculate independently using market data — no customer input required

Same measurement regardless of who is asked

Used when cost savings or performance differences are quantifiable in dollars or ratings
Perceived Customer Value
Derived from customer perceptions — must be measured from customers directly

Cannot be calculated without customer input — internal estimates are systematically biased

Ratings vary by segment — perception IS the reality for that customer

Used when product differences are felt but difficult to quantify in dollars
The 8% / 80% Gap — Gate 1

Only 8% of customers rate their experience as superior — yet 80% of companies believe they deliver a superior experience. This gap exists because companies rely on internal measurement. Perceived value requires external customer data. Ch. 4, p. 115.
