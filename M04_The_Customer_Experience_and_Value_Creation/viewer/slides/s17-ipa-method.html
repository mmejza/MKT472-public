IPA Method: Three Steps to Perceived Value
Importance-Performance Analysis — three data inputs, one value picture
1
Rate Importance — Constant Sum
Customers divide 100 importance points among product attributes — forces explicit tradeoffs between competing benefits. Avoids "everything is important" inflation.
2
Rate Performance — 0 to 10 Scale
Customers rate your product AND each competitor on each attribute (0 = disastrous, 10 = outstanding). Rating your own product alone produces biased comparisons.
3
Apply Threshold Rule — Binary, No Partial Credit
>2 points difference (i.e., ≥2 points): earn full importance points
≤1 point difference (including ties): earn zero importance points
This is NOT partial credit — the threshold is binary. Gate 2 tests this exactly.
Why Perceived Value Requires IPA

When product features converge across competitors, perceived performance and service quality become the primary differentiators. IPA captures these perceptions at the attribute level — revealing where the firm has a meaningful advantage and where it is vulnerable.
