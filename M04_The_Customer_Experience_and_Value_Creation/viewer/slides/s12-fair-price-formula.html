Fair Price: Price-Performance Ratio Method
Fair price estimates what the market implicitly says a product at this performance level should cost
Fair Price Formula
Fair Price = Performance Rating × (Avg Market Price ÷ Avg Performance Rating)
Customer Value = Fair Price − Actual Price  (positive = superior value zone)
Avg Market Price
Sum of all product prices in the market ÷ number of products
Avg Performance Rating
Sum of all performance ratings ÷ number of products in the market
Price/Performance Ratio
Avg market price ÷ avg performance rating = dollars per point of performance
Fair Price
Target product's performance rating × price-performance ratio
Two Things to Notice

1. This method produces a different fair price than regression — neither is "correct"; both estimate the market's implicit price-value relationship.
2. Gate 2 tests this calculation with parameterized inputs — practice the formula with the digital camera data on the next slide.
