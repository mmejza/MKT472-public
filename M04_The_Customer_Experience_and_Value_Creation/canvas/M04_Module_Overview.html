The Central Question
What this module asks you to figure out
Is Apex Industrial's FilterPro X7 correctly priced — or is the firm either leaving money on the table or asking customers to pay more than the product is worth?

This module gives you four measurement tools to close that gap: economic value, relative performance value, perceived value, and emotional value. Each one reveals something the others miss. Your case analysis will answer that question with calculations. This page prepares you for the work ahead.

Customer Reality

Only 8% of customers describe their experience as superior.

→
Company Belief

80% of companies believe they deliver a superior experience.

What this means: That gap is not primarily an execution failure. It is a measurement failure. Companies are not evaluating what customers actually experience — they are evaluating what they believe they deliver.

The Two-Gate Model

This module uses a two-gate reading accountability system. Here is how it works and why.

Gate 1
Basic Reading Check
  • Unlocks: Slide Deck Viewer
  • 5 questions · Chapter only · 70% threshold · 2 attempts · 0 points
  • Tests content present in Chapter 4 but absent from slides and script.
  • If you rely on slides alone, you will not pass Gate 1.
Gate 2
Applied Reading Check
  • Unlocks: Lecture Video
  • 8 questions · Chapter + slides · 1 attempt · 20 points
  • Requires synthesis: you need both the chapter and the slide deck.
  • Numeric questions use parameterized inputs — no two versions share the same correct answer.
!
Why two gates? A single gate before the lecture creates one gap: students who score just above the threshold proceed without genuine chapter internalization. Gate 1 certifies you read before viewing slides. Gate 2 certifies you connected the reading to the slides before watching the lecture. Students who game Gate 1 cannot game Gate 2 — the applied questions require both sources.

Two Traps to Watch For

This module contains two specific calculation errors that students make frequently. Both are addressed explicitly in the lecture video and the apps. Know them before you start.

Trap 1 — The Cost of Purchase Index Sign

The Cost of Purchase Index uses the same calculation method as the product performance indexes — same threshold rule, same averaging. But the sign runs in the OPPOSITE direction.

A relative advantage score of +27 on Purchase Price means customers rate your price as HIGHER than competitors. That is a competitive DISadvantage — not an advantage.

Where to find it: Lecture Segment 2 catches this error on screen. App 2 triggers a SIGN CHECK alert when you make it.

Trap 2 — CVI Does Not Equal Profit

The conjoint analysis produces a Customer Value Index (CVI) for each strategy. A higher CVI means more customer value. It does not mean more profit.

The strategy that charges more may generate a lower CVI (because buyers prefer lower prices) but more firm profit (because the premium exceeds the customer value gap).

Where to find it: Lecture Segment 1 plants this question. Segment 2 resolves it. App 3 surfaces it after you calculate both CVIs.
