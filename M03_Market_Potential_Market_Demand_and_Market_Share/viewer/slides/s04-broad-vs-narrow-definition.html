Broad vs. Narrow Market Definition
The same sales volume reads very differently depending on how the market is defined
Narrow Definition
· Defined by product category
· Excludes substitute products
· Smaller denominator
· Share appears larger
Example
Company A sells $500M in energy drink shots → 48% share of the energy shot sub-category
Broad Definition
· Defined by customer need
· Includes all substitutable products
· Larger denominator
· Share appears smaller
Same Example
Same $500M → 2.1% share of all packaged beverages (energy drinks, water, juice, soda)
Core LO1 Argument

Neither definition is wrong — but each reveals different things. A narrow definition hides the competitive threat from substitutes. A broad definition hides your strength within a committed niche. The choice of market definition is a strategic decision, not a measurement decision.
