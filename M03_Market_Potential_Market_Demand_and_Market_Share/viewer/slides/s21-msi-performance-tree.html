Market Share Performance Tree
Five sequential nodes — a customer lost at any node never reaches the next one
Node 1
Awareness
% of potential buyers who know the product exists
Largest leakage node (typically)
Node 2
Feature Attractiveness
% of aware buyers who find the product's features appealing
Node 3
Price Acceptable
% of attracted buyers who find the price acceptable
Node 4
Intentions to Buy
% who report intent to purchase in the near term
Node 5
Purchase
% of intenders who actually buy (conversion rate)
Smallest leakage (typically)
MSI = Product of All Five Rates

MSI = Node 1 × Node 2 × Node 3 × Node 4 × Node 5. A weak performance at Node 1 (awareness) reduces every downstream node — regardless of how strong the product features are.

Leakage = Where the Customers Go

The customers who fail each node are "leaked" — they exit the purchase pipeline. The node with the highest leakage is the highest-priority investment target.
