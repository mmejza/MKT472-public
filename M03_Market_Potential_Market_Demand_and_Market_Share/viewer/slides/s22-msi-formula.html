Market Share Index: Formula
MSI measures what the customer conversion pipeline is actually producing
MSI = Awareness × Feature % × Price % × Intent % × Purchase %
What It Measures
MSI — the fraction of the potential market the firm actually captures, given current node performance rates.

All five rates are expressed as decimals and multiplied together.
Two Things to Notice
1 — MSI is multiplicative. A Node 1 (Awareness) rate of 50% means every subsequent node operates on only half the potential base — regardless of how high their rates are.

2 — MSI ≠ SPI. MSI uses actual rates; SPI uses desired (benchmark) rates. SDI = MSI ÷ SPI × 100.
Direction Rule

MSI alone is not actionable — it must be compared to SPI (Share Potential Index, using desired performance levels). A low MSI means the pipeline is leaking. The leakage formula identifies where to fix it. This is covered on the next two slides.
