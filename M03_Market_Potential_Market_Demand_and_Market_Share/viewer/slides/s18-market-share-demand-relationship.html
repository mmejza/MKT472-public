Market Share and Market Demand
Volume is the product of two separate forces — a company can gain volume while losing share
Volume Sold = Market Demand × Market Share
Market Demand
Total purchases by all buyers in the market. Driven by: awareness, need, affordability, availability. Market-level variable.
Market Share
This firm's fraction of total demand. Driven by: awareness, product performance, price, distribution. Firm-level variable.
Volume Sold
What the firm actually ships and invoices. Depends on both variables moving simultaneously. Can rise even as share falls.
Why This Matters

In a fast-growing market, a firm can report record revenues while its market share is actually declining. Volume growth does not confirm competitive health. You must track share and demand separately — and that is what MSI and MDI do.
