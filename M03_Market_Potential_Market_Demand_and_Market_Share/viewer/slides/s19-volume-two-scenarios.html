Revenue Grew — Does That Mean You're Winning?
Volume increase is not the same as competitive gain — always decompose the two
Year 1 Year 2 Change
Market Demand 10.0M units 15.0M units +50% ↑
Firm's Market Share 12.0% 9.0% −3 pts ↓
Volume Sold 1.20M units 1.35M units +12.5% ↑
Common but Wrong Conclusion
"Volume grew by 12.5% — we must be doing something right." → Wrong. The market grew 50% and this firm only captured 9% of that growth. The firm lost 3 points of share while the market was rising.
Correct Rule

Separate the market effect from the share effect. Volume growth driven by a rising market does not indicate competitive strength — it indicates the firm survived in a favorable environment. Only share growth confirms real competitive progress.
