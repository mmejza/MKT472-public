Adding Market Scope Changes Everything
SportEdge — the reveal: each definition answers a different strategic question
Narrow (48.1%) Tells You
· Dominant vs. direct substitutes
· Pricing power within the sub-category
· Distribution presence among shot buyers
· Does NOT reveal: threat from water, juice, coffee
Broad (2.1%) Tells You
· Tiny slice of total consumption occasion
· Large substitution threat exists
· Massive untapped growth ceiling
· Does NOT reveal: depth of loyalty in the niche
KEY REVEAL
A company reporting 48% market share may be describing competitive dominance or a self-serving market definition. Before accepting any share figure, identify the market definition used to calculate it.
Takeaway

Narrow definition is not dishonest — it may reflect intentional strategic focus. But strategy built on only the narrow view misses the competitive threat and growth ceiling visible only from the broader definition.
