Market Development Index
MDI measures how much of market potential has been converted to actual demand
MDI = (Market Demand ÷ Market Potential) × 100
Components
Market Demand — actual total purchases in the market (current period)
Market Potential — maximum possible demand (from chain-ratio)
Result — an index from 0 to 100
Direction Rule
MDI < 50 → market underdeveloped → invest in market development
MDI ≈ 50 → market half developed → balance both strategies
MDI > 50 → market well-developed → invest in share gains
Two Things to Notice

1 — MDI is expressed as an index (0–100), not a percentage. An MDI of 40 means 40% of market potential is being realized — 60% remains untapped.

2 — MDI tells you where the market is in its development cycle — it is the quantitative foundation of PLC stage diagnosis (covered at s16).
