Forces Restricting Market Development
Five forces shown here — Chapter 3 defines seven (Gate 1 tests the full list)
Awareness
Potential customers don't know the product exists or what problem it solves
Restricts: Q (buying ceiling)
Acceptability
Product features or design don't match customer values, habits, or needs
Restricts: Q (buying ceiling)
Affordability
Price exceeds what segments can or will pay — limits who can enter the market
Restricts: Q (buying ceiling)
Availability
Product isn't distributed where and when customers want to buy it
Restricts: P (purchase rate)
Willingness
Customers know about and can afford the product but choose not to purchase
Restricts: Q (buying ceiling)
