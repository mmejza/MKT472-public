Market Potential: Chain-Ratio Formula
Maximum demand = the product of five market conditions — each factor multiplies the next
Market Potential = N × Q × P × F × Price
Components
N — Number of potential consuming units (households, individuals, firms)
Q — Buying ceiling (% who would ever purchase under ideal conditions)
P — Annual purchase rate (times per year)
F — Quantity per purchase occasion
Price — Avg. unit price (converts units to dollars)
Two Things to Notice
1 — Every factor is a multiplier. One bottleneck factor (e.g., low buying ceiling) limits the entire result — regardless of how large the other factors are.

2 — N × Q × P × F gives units. Multiply by Price to get dollar potential. Gate 2 asks for both — don't stop at units.
Binding Constraint Rule

The factor with the lowest rate controls market potential more than any other. Doubling a factor that is already near 100% produces little gain. Doubling the binding constraint can multiply total potential. Identify the bottleneck first.
