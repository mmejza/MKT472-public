Why Revenue Growth Can Hide Competitive Failure
Internal financial metrics and external market metrics can tell opposite stories
The Paradox
A company's revenue grows every year for three consecutive years. Net profit triples. Every internal metric looks excellent.
Meanwhile, the market it operates in is growing faster than the company. Every year, competitors take a larger share of an expanding pie. The company's competitive position is eroding — and no one on the management team knows it.
This is not unusual. It is the default outcome when managers rely on internal metrics alone.
Two Metric Types — Two Different Stories
Internal Metrics
Revenue · Net profit · EPS · Return on equity
Measure what happened inside the firm. Cannot show how the firm is performing relative to the market.
External Metrics
Market share · Customer satisfaction · NMC · Share of wallet
Measure the firm's competitive position in the marketplace. Reveal share erosion that revenue growth can hide.
The Core Diagnostic Problem

Internal metrics are necessary but not sufficient. A company that tracks only internal results is driving with its eyes on the dashboard — not the road. Net Marketing Contribution (NMC) bridges both worlds: it is financially derived (credible to the C-suite) and market-oriented (sensitive to competitive position).
