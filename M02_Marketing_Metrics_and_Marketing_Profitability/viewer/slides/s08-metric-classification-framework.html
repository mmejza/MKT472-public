Metric Classification: The 2×2 Framework
Internal vs. External × Backward-Looking vs. Forward-Looking
Internal · Backward-Looking
Measures what happened inside the firm. Useful for reporting and accountability — not for anticipating competitive threats.
Example: Net Profit · Revenue · Gross Margin
Internal · Forward-Looking
Measures internal indicators that predict future performance. Useful for planning — still does not measure competitive position.
Example: Employee Satisfaction · R&D Pipeline · Order Backlog
External · Backward-Looking
Measures the firm's competitive position after the fact. More useful than internal metrics — but still reports on what already happened in the market.
Example: Market Share · Customer Retention · NMC
External · Forward-Looking ★
Measures external leading indicators of competitive position. Highest strategic value — signals threats and opportunities before they reach financial statements.
Example: Intent to Repurchase · Customer Satisfaction · Relative Market Share
Classification Rule

Ask two questions: (1) Does this metric measure something inside the firm or in the marketplace? (2) Does it report on what has already happened, or signal what is about to happen? External + forward-looking metrics give management the earliest warning of competitive deterioration. Practice the classification at the next slide.
