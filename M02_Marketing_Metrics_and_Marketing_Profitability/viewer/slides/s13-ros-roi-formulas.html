Marketing ROS and Marketing ROI: Formulas
Two ratios — different denominators, different strategic questions
Marketing Return on Sales
Marketing ROS = NMC ÷ Revenue × 100
Denominator = Revenue → scale-neutral. Compares marketing productivity across businesses or divisions of different sizes without being distorted by revenue differences.
Marketing Return on Investment
Marketing ROI = NMC ÷ MSE × 100
Denominator = MSE → spending efficiency. Asks: for each dollar invested in the marketing program, how many dollars of NMC are returned? Floor threshold = 100%.
ProLine (from slide 12)
NMC = $21M · Revenue = $60M · MSE = $15M

Marketing ROS = $21M ÷ $60M × 100 = 35.0%
Marketing ROI = $21M ÷ $15M × 100 = 140%
Reading the Results
ROS = 35% → 35 cents of NMC for every $1 of revenue
ROI = 140% → $1.40 of NMC for every $1 spent on marketing

ROI above 100% confirms the program is profitable. See s14 for the floor rule.
Denominator Is the Key

Both ratios use NMC in the numerator. The denominator determines what question is being answered: ROS asks "how productive is each revenue dollar?" — ROI asks "how efficient is each marketing dollar?" Gate 2 Q2 tests ROS winner; Q3 tests the ROI floor. Both are on the next slide.
