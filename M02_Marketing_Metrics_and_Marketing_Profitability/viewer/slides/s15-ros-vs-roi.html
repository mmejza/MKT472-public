ROS vs. ROI: Which Metric for Which Decision
Same NMC — different lenses for different strategic questions
Use Marketing ROS when…
✓ Comparing two or more divisions, segments, or product lines with different revenue sizes

✓ Answering: "Which business generates more NMC per revenue dollar?"

✓ Building a multi-year trend showing marketing productivity over time
Gate 2 Q2: Which company (Alpha or Beta) has higher Marketing ROS?
Use Marketing ROI when…
✓ Evaluating whether a marketing budget is efficiently deployed

✓ Answering: "Does each dollar of MSE recover more than its cost?"

✓ Deciding whether to increase, hold, or cut the marketing spend
Gate 2 Q3: Is the marketing program above or below the 100% floor?
The Key Distinction

A large-revenue division can have a lower Marketing ROS than a small one — ROS corrects for scale. But the same large division may have a higher Marketing ROI if it runs a leaner marketing budget per NMC dollar. The two ratios answer different questions and should never be used interchangeably. Misidentifying which ratio to use is the most common strategic error this module tests.
