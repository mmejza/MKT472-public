Net Marketing Contribution: Formula
NMC makes marketing's profit contribution visible and financially defensible
NMC = Gross Profit − Marketing & Sales Expense (MSE)
Components
Gross Profit = Revenue − Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)

MSE = Marketing & Sales Expense — the direct cost of creating and executing the marketing program

The 75% Rule — when MSE is not separately reported:
MSE ≈ 75% of SGA
Direction Rule
NMC > 0 → marketing program is profitable; gross profit exceeds marketing costs

NMC < 0 → marketing program destroys value; marketing costs exceed the gross profit generated

NMC = 0 is break-even — the program recovers its cost but creates no incremental profit.
Two Things to Notice

1 — MSE is subtracted from gross profit, not from revenue. Operating overhead (G&A) is deliberately excluded — NMC measures marketing's direct contribution before fixed corporate costs are allocated.

2 — The 75% Rule applies when the income statement reports a combined SGA line. Research on industry averages shows roughly 75% of SGA is Marketing & Sales; the remaining 25% is G&A. Gate 2 Q1 uses this rule.
