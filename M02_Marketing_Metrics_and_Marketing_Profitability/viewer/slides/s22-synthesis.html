What NMC Reveals That Net Profit Conceals
Three strategic contexts — one metric that works in all three
Context 1 — Competitive Position (LO1–LO2)
Net profit says the company is healthy. Market compounding and NMC reveal that revenue growth is slower than the market — share is declining without any internal alarm going off.
External + forward-looking metrics are the only early warning system.
Context 2 — Profitability Decisions (LO3–LO4)
Net profit says drop the losing line. NMC says keep it — it is covering fixed overhead that will not leave with the line. Operating income says one thing; NMC says the opposite.
Use NMC for drop decisions. Use ROS or ROI for comparing spending efficiency.
Context 3 — Budget Allocation (LO5)
Two divisions need budget. One has higher absolute NMC; the other has higher Marketing ROS. Marketing ROI shows which program is the more efficient use of the next dollar.
ROS for comparing divisions. ROI for deciding whether to increase the budget.
NMC as the Course Thread

Every module in MKT 472 from M05 (segment targeting) to M06 (competitive position) uses NMC as the primary decision criterion. Fluency with all five uses — as a diagnostic (LO1), a classifier (LO2), a calculation (LO3), a drop-decision tool (LO4), and a lever framework (LO5) — is the foundation for every quantitative case in this course.
