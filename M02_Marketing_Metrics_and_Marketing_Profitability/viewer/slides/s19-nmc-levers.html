The NMC Levers: Expanded Formula
Each factor in the NMC chain is a lever management can pull
NMC = Market Demand × Market Share × Revenue/Customer × (1 − Variable Cost %) − MSE
Lever 1
Market Demand
Total potential customers in the served market
Grow via market development
Lever 2
Market Share
% of market demand captured by the firm
Grow via share development
Lever 3
Revenue/Customer
Average revenue per acquired customer (price)
Grow via price premium
Lever 4
Variable Cost %
% of revenue consumed by variable production costs
Improve via cost reduction
Lever 5
MSE
Marketing & Sales Expense — the direct cost of the program
Improve via efficiency gains
Mechanical Advantage — Why Earlier Levers Matter More

Levers 1–3 (demand, share, price) are multiplicative — a 10% improvement in any one compounds through the entire downstream chain. Lever 5 (MSE) is additive — cutting it by $1M saves exactly $1M. Gate 2 Q6 compares two strategies and asks which produces the higher NMC. Always calculate both — don't guess based on which lever "sounds" bigger.
