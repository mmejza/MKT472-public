Market Compounding: Formula
Revenue must grow as fast as the market — or share erodes
Market(t) = Market₀ × (1 + g)t
Components
Market(t) — market size at the end of Year t
Market₀ — current (base year) market size
g — annual market growth rate (decimal)
t — number of years forward

Required revenue = Market(t) × firm's market share %
Direction Rule
Firm revenue grows ≥ market → share maintained or gained

Firm revenue grows < market → share erodes, even as revenue rises

This is the mechanism behind the paradox on slide 4: growing profit, declining position.
Two-Step Application

Step 1: Calculate market size at Year t using the formula above. Step 2: Multiply Market(t) by the firm's current share percentage to find the revenue target required to maintain that share. The gap between this target and the firm's actual revenue trajectory is the competitive warning signal internal metrics cannot provide.
