Drop Paradox: Solution
ProLine Fitness Equipment — New NMC = $6.8M · Company is $1.2M worse off
Before Drop After Drop (CasualMat gone) Change
Total Company NMC $8.0M $6.8M −$1.2M
Fixed Overhead $7.0M $7.0M unchanged
Net Income $1.0M −$0.2M −$1.2M
The Calculation
New NMC = $8.0M − $1.2M = $6.8M

The $1.2M is CasualMat's NMC — not its net profit (−$0.7M). Do not add back $0.7M. Fixed overhead of $7.0M stays on the books.
The Correct Decision
Keep CasualMat. Despite its −$0.7M net profit, it generates +$1.2M of NMC that absorbs fixed overhead. Dropping it turns a $1.0M profit company into a −$0.2M loss company — the opposite of the intended result.
Bottom Rule — NMC Is the Drop Decision Metric

A product line with positive NMC must be retained, regardless of its net profit sign. Net profit includes allocated fixed overhead that does not disappear when the line is dropped. Only NMC measures the direct contribution a line makes to covering those fixed costs.
