The Measurement Problem
More impressions do not automatically translate into sales
More impressions = more sales.
Why it seems correct
  • Reach and frequency are easy to buy and easy to report
  • GRPs go up when spend goes up — the metric moves predictably
  • Awareness is a genuine precondition for purchase
Why it is wrong
  • Awareness does not guarantee comprehension, intention, or purchase
  • A campaign can reach 90% of the target market and produce a CRI below 2%
  • The CRI shows that each stage multiplies the prior stage — a 28% comprehension rate collapses the whole funnel regardless of reach
Correct rule

Evaluate communications at every stage of the customer response hierarchy, not just at the top. The weakest stage — not the lowest spend — determines the CRI ceiling.
