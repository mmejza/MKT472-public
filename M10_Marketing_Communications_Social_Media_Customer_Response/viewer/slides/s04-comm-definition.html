Marketing Communications
A communication is only effective when its objective and metric match
A marketing communication is only justifiably effective when it is designed around a measurable customer-response objective — not around creative quality, reach, or spend.
Three communication objectives
Brand-Image Brand-Information Brand-Action
Example: VW "The Force" — Super Bowl XLV
  • Posted 4 days pre-game → 10M YouTube views, 62K likes before the ad aired
  • Game-day broadcast → estimated 100M impressions
  • Post-game → additional 18.5M views
  • Objective: brand-image — awareness and emotional association, not direct response
Why it matters

Objective determines metric. A brand-image ad measured by click-through rate is being evaluated on the wrong standard. Mismatched objectives and metrics guarantee apparent failure.
