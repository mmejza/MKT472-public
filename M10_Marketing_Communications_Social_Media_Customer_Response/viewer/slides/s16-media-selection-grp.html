Media Selection: GRP and CPM
Media efficiency and customer response are related but different problems
Gross Rating Points
GRP = Reach% × Frequency
Example: 60% reach × 4 frequency = 240 GRPs
Cost per Thousand (CPM)
CPM = (Media Cost ÷ Impressions) × 1,000
Lower CPM = more efficient reach per dollar
Two things to notice

GRPs measure combined impact — a 60-reach/4-frequency plan and a 80-reach/3-frequency plan both produce 240 GRPs, but serve different strategic goals: breadth vs. depth

CRI warning

GRP is not CRI — high GRP with weak comprehension still produces a low CRI; GRP only controls the Exposure and Awareness stages

Decision rule

Use GRP to compare media plans on reach efficiency. Use CRI to evaluate whether that reach is converting. Both are required — neither is sufficient alone.
