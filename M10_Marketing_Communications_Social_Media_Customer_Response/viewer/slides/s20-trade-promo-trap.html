Key Reveal
The supplier's month-2 volume spike (16,000 units) was driven by retailer forward-buying at the discount price — not by incremental consumer demand. The retailer bought less before the promotion, over-bought during it, and bought far less after. The supplier's 3-month gross profit fell $1,200 below baseline.
Takeaway
A promotion that lifts volume can still destroy supplier margin. Push spend must be evaluated on 3-month profit — not on promotion-month sales. The metric that makes the promotion look successful is the one most likely to mislead.
