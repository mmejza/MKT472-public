Advertising Elasticity
Advertising is usually a weak short-run sales lever, not an immediate conversion machine
Advertising Elasticity
EA = % Change in Sales ÷ % Change in Ad Spend
Industry benchmark
  • Study of 128 advertising elasticities: average = 0.22
  • Few elasticities exceed 0.50 — advertising is a weak short-run sales lever relative to price
  • Hart Schaffner Marx estimate: 0.50 (2.4% sales change ÷ 4.8% spend change)
Two things to notice
  • Elasticity is small by design — advertising builds awareness and preference over time, not immediate sales response; judging it on short-run ROI alone systematically undervalues it
  • Elasticity varies by market share — brands with high market share have lower elasticity; incremental reach becomes harder and more expensive as coverage grows
Decision rule

EA below 0.22 signals below-average response — investigate whether the problem is copy, audience, or frequency before cutting budget. Cutting spend is a symptom treatment, not a diagnosis.
