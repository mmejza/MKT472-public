Background

Apex Audio sells premium wireless speakers in a competitive consumer electronics market. The brand has high awareness (65%) but low comprehension (38%) — most buyers who know the brand cannot articulate why it commands a premium. Year-over-year sales are flat. The CFO and the Brand Manager are in disagreement about what to do with the marketing budget this quarter.

CFO — Cut Media Budget, Run Trade Promotion

“We need short-term revenue and we need it now. I want to redirect 30% of the media budget into a distributor trade promotion for one quarter. Month-two volume will prove this works. If the brand equity takes a small hit, we can restore it later once we’ve stabilized the P&L. Awareness is already strong at 65% — we don’t need more reach, we need more product moving off shelves.”

Brand Manager — Fix Comprehension First

“We have an awareness-to-comprehension gap: 65% awareness but only 38% comprehension. That gap is where we’re losing buyers. Comprehension is the highest-leverage CRI stage — a 10-point gain there yields a 1.12-point purchase rate gain, more than any other stage. A trade promotion will trigger forward-buying from distributors and suppress 3-month gross profit below our baseline. The carryover math also shows that strong current-period media spend compounds into future periods. We should protect the media budget and address the comprehension gap directly.”

Part 1 — Your Position

Take a clear position — side with either the CFO (cut media budget, run trade promotion) or the Brand Manager (protect media budget and fix comprehension). Using the CRI leverage data and the 3-month gross profit figures, defend your position in 150–200 words. Reference specific numbers — do not just describe the argument in general terms.

Part 2 — Devil's Advocate

Now make the strongest case for the position you just argued against. What is the best evidence the other side has? Where does your own CRI or GP argument have a weakness? What conditions would have to be true for the opposing side to be right? (100–150 words)

What Strong Posts Look Like

The best posts name the specific CRI stage with the largest drop, identify why that stage is highest-leverage using the marginal gain data, and compare the trade promotion’s 3-month gross profit ($852,900) to baseline ($888,000) rather than just asserting one side “wins.” If you defend the CFO, you need to explain why a short-term volume spike justifies a $35,100 GP shortfall and how you plan to recover carryover effects. If you defend the Brand Manager, name the specific leverage figure (1.12pp) and explain what the GRP trap implies about high-spending-low-comprehension scenarios. Either way, your devil's advocate must engage the data, not just restate the scenario.

Connection to the Case Analysis

This discussion surfaces the same communications investment decision you will analyze formally in Case Parts 2–3 (CRI diagnosis and channel allocation) and Case Part 4 (strategic recommendation). The arguments you develop here — about CRI stage leverage, trade promotion GP effects, and carryover dynamics — are directly useful when you build your case recommendation. Students who engage seriously with this debate write stronger case analyses.
