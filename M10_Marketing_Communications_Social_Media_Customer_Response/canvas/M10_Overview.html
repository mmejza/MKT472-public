MKT 472  ·  Module 10

Marketing Communications, Social Media, and Customer Response

Chapter 10  ·  ~4.5–5.5 hours total  ·  Summer session: plan ~5 hours for this module

Central Question
Is a promotion that lifts sales always profitable for the supplier? The answer will surprise you — and it changes how you evaluate every push communications decision you make.

What This Module Builds

This module is about measurement discipline in marketing communications. You'll learn that reach and impressions are easy to buy — but they measure the wrong thing. What matters is the Customer Response Index: a five-stage hierarchy that shows exactly where a communications program is failing and where the budget should move.

You'll also learn that a trade promotion that produces a volume spike in month two can simultaneously destroy the supplier's gross profit over the three-month window — because the spike is driven by distributor forward-buying, not consumer demand. The numbers look exactly right until you look at the full picture.

What makes this module hard
Three calculations in this module produce counterintuitive results. The CRI diagnostic shows that the lowest-performing stage is not always the highest-leverage fix. The carryover formula shows that short-run ROI understates advertising value by nearly 2×. The trade promotion analysis shows that the CFO's favorite metric — monthly volume — is the one most likely to mislead. You need to work through all three before the case makes sense.

Complete through Gate 2 on day one; apps and discussion on day two; case analysis on day three. The discussion deadline is set three days before the case deadline — the arguments you develop there are the same arguments you'll need to resolve quantitatively in the case.
