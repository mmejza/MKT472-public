Why Channels Exist: Three Functions
Channel economics are driven by function efficiency, not intermediary count

Quantity

Quantity discrepancy adjustment — manufacturers produce in large batches; end-users buy in small quantities. Intermediaries break bulk.

Ford produces vehicles in plants → dealers sell one at a time to individuals

Assortment

Assortment discrepancy adjustment — manufacturers specialize in narrow product lines; buyers want variety in one place. Intermediaries aggregate.

Supermarket stocks 40,000 SKUs from thousands of manufacturers → customer buys 30 items in one trip

Contactual

Contactual efficiency — direct manufacturer-to-buyer contact creates millions of transactions; intermediaries collapse the contact count dramatically.

100 manufacturers × 20,000 retailers = 24M contacts/yr → with wholesalers = 241,200 contacts/yr

Services

Additional value-added services — risk assumption, credit extension, sorting, selling effort, fulfillment, technical support.

Distributor carries inventory risk; retailer provides sales staff and returns processing

Takeaway

Intermediaries exist because they perform functions more efficiently than manufacturers can — including reducing transaction costs by 82.5% (Ch. 9, Figs. 9-21/22).
