Sales Force Sizing
Formula Reps needed = (Customers × Visits/month × 12) ÷ (Visits/day × Days/week × Weeks/year)

Worked example — Ch. 9

1
Total annual visits required
1,000 customers × 2 visits/month × 12 = 24,000 visits/year
2
Annual capacity per rep
3 visits/day × 4 days/week × 50 weeks = 600 visits/year
3
Reps needed
24,000 ÷ 600 = 40 salespeople
Competitive edge
If competitors average 20 reps, they cover 50% of customers or visit at half the frequency — either way, a structural service quality disadvantage.

Two things to notice

  • Sizing is a coverage decision — not just a cost decision. Underfunding headcount directly reduces customer reach and service quality (two of the three channel performance pillars).
  • Competitive advantage is quantifiable — a firm with 40 reps vs. a competitor with 20 can double the contact frequency or double the customer coverage. Both create measurable share advantage.
  • Gate 2 Q6 requires computing this from different customer count, visit frequency, and rep capacity inputs. Know the three-step structure.
