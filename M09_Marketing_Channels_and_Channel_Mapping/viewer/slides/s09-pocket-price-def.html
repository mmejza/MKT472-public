Pocket Price

The revenue the manufacturer actually receives per unit after all channel deductions — retailer discounts, distributor discounts, agent commissions, and transaction costs — have been subtracted from the end-user price.

The waterfall structure

1
Start: End-user price — what the customer pays
2
Deduct: Retailer discount % → wholesale price
3
Deduct: Distributor discount % (on wholesale price)
4
Deduct: Agent commission % (where applicable)
5
Deduct: Transaction cost % → Pocket price

Why it matters

  • Two channels with the same end-user price can produce radically different pocket prices
  • Pocket price is the revenue base for computing margin, NMC, and ROI
  • The manufacturer cannot change the end-user price without affecting demand — but channel structure directly determines pocket price
  • A seemingly minor change in discount structure (e.g., 15% → 20% retailer discount) compounds through the waterfall
