Marketing Channels

The conduits that connect a company's products to its end-user customers — determining who touches the product, who owns it at each stage, and what portion of the end-user price the manufacturer ultimately receives.

What channels do
  • Create customer access — make products available at preferred purchase points
  • Adjust quantity discrepancies — manufacturers produce large lots; buyers need small quantities
  • Adjust assortment discrepancies — manufacturers make narrow lines; buyers want variety
  • Add transaction efficiency — consolidate contacts between producers and buyers
Strategic implication
  • Without channels, no sale is possible — regardless of product quality or price
  • Channel choice determines pocket price — what the manufacturer actually receives
  • Channel choice determines Marketing ROI — not just margin
  • Channel systems can be a source of competitive advantage — or a structural cost problem
Core idea Channel strategy is a profitability architecture decision — it determines revenue, cost, and ROI before any unit ships.
