Direct vs. Indirect vs. Mixed Channel Systems

Three system types — different control, cost, and profitability profiles

Direct
Control Maximum — business owns all customer touchpoints
Pocket price Highest — no intermediary discounts
M&S cost Highest — business funds all selling
ROI Often lowest — high cost base
Examples Sales force, direct mail, telemarketing, digital
Indirect
Control Lowest — intermediaries interface with customers
Pocket price Lowest — multiple discount layers
M&S cost Lowest — intermediaries absorb selling cost
ROI Often highest — minimal M&S investment
Examples Wholesalers → retailers, distributors → dealers
Mixed
Control Partial — direct for large accounts, indirect for small
Pocket price Varies by channel segment
M&S cost Intermediate — cost shared with intermediaries
ROI Depends on mix — optimized by segment
Examples HP (all three channels), Caterpillar, Microsoft
Bottom takeaway No system type is universally superior. The right system depends on customer segment, purchase size, service requirement, and cost structure — not on a preference for "control."
