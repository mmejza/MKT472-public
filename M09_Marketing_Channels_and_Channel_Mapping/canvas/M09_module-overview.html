Central question for this module

"If the direct channel delivers higher margins and higher NMC than the indirect channel — why would any firm ever choose indirect?"

The answer is not obvious. By the end of this module you will have computed the pocket price waterfall across three channel configurations, calculated NMC and Marketing ROI for each, and discovered why the channel with the highest margin can simultaneously have the lowest return on marketing investment. That reversal — the channel ROI paradox — is the analytical core of this module and the foundation of the NovaTech case.

Learning Objectives
LO1
Application
Compute pocket price across a multi-tier channel by tracing the full waterfall of discounts, commissions, and transaction costs.
LO2
Analysis
Calculate NMC, Marketing ROS, and Marketing ROI; explain why the highest-margin channel can have the lowest ROI.
LO3
Evaluation
Evaluate whether a channel strategy switch is justified by computing break-even market share and comparing it to current share.
LO4
Analysis
Distinguish direct, indirect, and mixed channel systems by cost structure, control, and profitability profile.
