Marketing Channels and Channel Mapping

How channel structure determines what you actually receive.

Part III · Marketing Program Design 165 points Two-Gate Accountability Reduced Workload Module
Central Question
What this module asks you to figure out
If the direct channel delivers higher margins and higher NMC than the indirect channel - why would any firm ever choose indirect?

The answer is not obvious. By the end of this module you will have computed the pocket price waterfall across three channel configurations, calculated NMC and Marketing ROI for each, and discovered why the channel with the highest margin can simultaneously have the lowest return on marketing investment.

That reversal - the channel ROI paradox - is the analytical core of this module and the foundation of the NovaTech case.

!
Strategic Framing

Do not assume channel margin and channel ROI point to the same decision. This module is built around that contradiction.

Two Traps to Watch For

These two errors appear repeatedly in gate responses, app interpretation, and NovaTech recommendations.

Assuming higher margin and higher NMC automatically mean higher ROI

The channel ROI paradox is the module core: a channel can produce higher margin and higher NMC but still deliver lower Marketing ROI because ROI depends on the M&S expense base.

Where it appears: Gate 2 numeric items, App 1 ROI paradox prompt, and the NovaTech recommendation logic.

Using the wrong data source for the decision

Chapter examples, slides, lecture scenarios, app presets, and case data serve different purposes. Importing numbers across them leads to invalid conclusions.

Where it appears: App worksheets and case analysis. Keep NovaTech values separate from chapter and app defaults.
