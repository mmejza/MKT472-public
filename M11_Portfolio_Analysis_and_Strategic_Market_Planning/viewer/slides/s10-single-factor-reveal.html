Adding strategy logic to both dimensions — not just growth rate — changes the answer for Product E.

Product Growth SDI Recommended Strategy Rationale
A 5% 84 Protect Position Near share ceiling; manage margin, protect profitability
B 0% 65 Harvest Share No growth; extract remaining profit, then exit
C 10% 50 Grow Share Growth stage + 50% share potential untapped — invest now
D 20% 32 Grow Share High growth + 68% potential remaining = highest priority investment
E ⚑ 23% 90 Hold Share Fast growth BUT 90% of potential captured — investing to grow beyond this yields diminishing returns
Key reveal
Product E (fastest-growing market) → Hold Share, not Grow Share. SDI = 90 means share potential is nearly exhausted. Growth rate alone would recommend "Invest to Grow" for E — and be wrong.
Takeaway
Two factors determine strategy. A single-factor model misclassifies Product E and misallocates investment. This is why multi-factor analysis exists.
