Two Approaches to Portfolio Visualization
LO 1 Contrast 06 / 21
Price-Quality Portfolio
  • Maps products by price point and quality / brand status
  • Reveals positioning gaps and competitive white space
  • Shows where the portfolio is concentrated or missing
Example

Toyota: 3 models in low-price tier (1960s) → 18 models across 3 brands spanning full price-quality curve (2010)

Product Life Cycle Portfolio
  • Maps products by PLC stage and market growth rate
  • Circle size = current sales volume
  • Balanced = mix of emerging, growth, and mature stages
  • Unbalanced = all products concentrated in mature or declining
Risk signal

A portfolio showing only mature/late-growth products looks profitable today — but has no pipeline. Revenue cliff follows in 3–5 years.

Bottom takeaway
Both models are inputs to portfolio analysis — neither alone determines strategy. Life-cycle position sets market attractiveness; competitive position requires a separate index. Slides 12–17 build both.
