Strategic Market Plan vs. Marketing Mix Strategy
LO 2 Contrast 20 / 21
Strategic Market Plan
Time horizon 3–5 years
Sets objectives for Market share position · Sales growth · Long-run NMC
Review cycle Annually against long-run trajectory
Resource scope Full portfolio investment allocation
Example

"Grow Beta's market share from 10% to 16% and raise NMC from $12.8M to $24M over 3 years through an Improve Position strategy."

Marketing Mix Strategy
Time horizon 1 year
Specifies tactics for Product positioning · Pricing · Communications · Channels
Review cycle Quarterly; adjusted when market conditions shift
Resource scope Annual marketing and sales budget for one product
Example

"Reduce Beta's price 5% and increase digital ad spend by $400K in Q1 to accelerate trial in the B2B segment."

Bottom takeaway
Strategy sets the destination — market share and NMC targets 3–5 years out. Marketing mix is how you navigate to it: the 4Ps adjusted year-by-year as market conditions change. The wrong strategic plan cannot be rescued by a brilliant mix. The right strategic plan can survive an imperfect mix if course-corrected in time.
