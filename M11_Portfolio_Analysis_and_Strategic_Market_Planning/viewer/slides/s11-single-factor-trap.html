"High Market Growth Rate = Attractive Market"
LO 2 Misconception 11 / 21
Common but wrong
"If the market is growing fast, it must be attractive — invest."
Why it seems correct
  • Growth → rising demand → more sales opportunity
  • Product D (20% growth) clearly warrants more attention than Product B (0%)
  • Fast-growing markets produce the biggest future profit pools
Why it is wrong
  • Growth ignores market size — 20% growth on $10M < 5% growth on $5B
  • Growth ignores competitive intensity — high growth attracts entrants, compressing margin
  • Growth ignores market access — distribution barriers can make growth unreachable
Correct rule
Market attractiveness requires a multi-factor index across market forces, competitive environment, and market access. Growth rate is one of nine inputs — not the answer.
