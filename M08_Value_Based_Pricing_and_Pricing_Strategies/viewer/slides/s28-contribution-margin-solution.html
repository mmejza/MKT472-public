Q2 Insight

Raising price while cutting VC simultaneously amplifies margin improvement. The 10% price increase alone would give CM = $50 (50%). Adding the VC reduction pushes it to $56 (50.9%).

Q3 Warning

A 10% VC increase without a price response reduces CM from $40 to $34 — a 15% margin decline on a 10% cost increase. Cost passthrough requires a price decision.

Key Takeaway

CM is the denominator in the BE% formula. A higher CM means a price change requires less volume response to break even — which is why margin discipline matters before any pricing decision.
