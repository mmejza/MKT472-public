The Problem with Cost-Driven Pricing
A circular logic trap — volume assumed to compute price, but price determines volume
The Cost-Based Formula

Target Price = (Total Costs ÷ Expected Volume) + Profit Margin

The firm assumes a sales volume, derives a unit cost, adds margin — then sets a price.

The Fatal Assumption

Expected volume is assumed before price is set. But price affects demand — which changes actual volume — which changes unit cost — which invalidates the original price.

Underpricing Risk

High-value customers would pay more. Cost-based price leaves money on the table by ignoring their willingness-to-pay.

Overpricing Risk

Price exceeds willingness-to-pay → volume collapses → unit cost rises → firm loses more margin than projected.
