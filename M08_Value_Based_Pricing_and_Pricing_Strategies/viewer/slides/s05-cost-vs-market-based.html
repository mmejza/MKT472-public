Cost-Based vs. Market-Based Pricing
The starting point determines the outcome
Cost-Driven Pricing
① Start with total costs
② Divide by assumed volume
③ Add desired profit margin
④ Set price
⑤ Hope customers buy
Market-Based Pricing
① Start with customer value
② Determine willingness-to-pay
③ Set price below value ceiling
④ Engineer costs to target
⑤ Profit follows by design
Bottom Takeaway

Cost-based pricing ignores what customers value — and assumes volume that the price itself may destroy. Market-based pricing starts where profit is actually created: in the customer's perception of value.
