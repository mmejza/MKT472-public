Is $2.20 the Right Price?
EV is the ceiling — not the target
Common but Wrong Conclusion

"We calculated EV = $2.20 per unit, so we should charge $2.20."

Why It Seems Correct

$2.20 is the maximum justifiable premium. The customer receives equal total cost either way — so the firm captures all value.

Why It Is Wrong

At EV = price, the customer is indifferent between switching and staying. Indifference is not a purchase decision. The firm needs to leave surplus value for the customer.

Correct Rule

Set price below EV so the customer captures a meaningful incentive to switch. The actual price depends on how much competitive pressure exists and how much margin the firm targets. EV analysis provides the ceiling — strategy determines the exact price.
