When to Use Penetration Pricing
Six conditions — contrast directly with skim
① Price Elastic Demand

Volume responds strongly to price reductions — a lower price generates disproportionately higher demand

② Not Unique

The product is not highly differentiated — customers see the firm's offer as broadly comparable to alternatives

③ Easy to Imitate

Competitors can enter quickly — early volume and scale become the only sustainable competitive barrier

④ No Distinct Segments

The market does not break into meaningful willingness-to-pay tiers — one price must serve most buyers

⑤ Large Scale Economics

Significant per-unit cost savings are achievable at high volume — high sales justify the low price

⑥ Market Share Objective

The primary goal is capturing share quickly — even at the cost of short-term margin — to achieve scale or lock in customers
