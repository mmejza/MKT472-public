The Value-Based Pricing Problem
Objective value ≠ perceived value — price must bridge both

Objective Value

Measurable economic benefit — cost savings, performance advantage, reliability. Independent of customer perception.

Perceived Value

What the customer believes the product is worth. Shaped by marketing, branding, competitive alternatives, and experience.

Price of Substitutes

The next-best alternative anchors the customer's reference price. Your price must beat the substitute's value — not just your cost.

Consumer Incentive to Buy

Perceived Value must exceed Price. The gap is the consumer surplus — the customer's reason to purchase.

Company Incentive to Sell

Price must exceed COGS. The gap is gross margin — the firm's reason to produce and market the product.

The Pricing Problem

Setting price requires balancing consumer surplus (to ensure purchase) with company margin (to ensure profitability). Cost-based pricing optimizes only one side of this equation.
