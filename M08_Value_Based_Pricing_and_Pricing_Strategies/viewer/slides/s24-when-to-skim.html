When to Use Skim Pricing
Six conditions — all should be present
① Price Inelastic Demand

Customers in the target segment are relatively insensitive to price — quantity demanded changes little when price rises

② Distinct Price-Market Segments

Clearly separated customer segments exist that differ meaningfully in willingness-to-pay

③ Hard to Imitate

The offering is protected — by patent, trade secret, proprietary technology, or a high-cost production process — from rapid competitive entry

④ Unknown Production Costs

Costs are uncertain at launch — skim generates early revenue and information before cost structure is optimized

⑤ Capacity Constraint

Production or service capacity limits volume — high price rations supply to the highest-value customers

⑥ Realistic Perceived Value

Customers genuinely believe the high price reflects superior value — the price premium is not seen as exploitative
