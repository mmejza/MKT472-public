Value-in-Use Pricing: Economic Value
EV sets the price ceiling — not the price floor
EV = TCONext-Best − TCOFirm's Product

TCO Components

Total Cost of Ownership = Operating costs + Expected failure costs + All other usage costs over the comparison period

Expected Failure Cost

= Failure probability × Cost per failure event
Captures risk, not just sticker price

What EV Represents

The maximum price premium the customer can rationally pay above the next-best alternative before switching is no longer rational

Direction Rule

EV > 0 → firm's product saves the customer money vs. the alternative → a price premium is justified

Pricing Rule

Set price below EV. The gap between price and EV is the customer's incentive to switch. Price = EV leaves zero incentive.
