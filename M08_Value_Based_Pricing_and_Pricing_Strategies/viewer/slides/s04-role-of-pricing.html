The Role of Pricing
Pricing captures value — it does not define value
Core Purpose

To capture some of the value created for the customer — in the form of revenue — so as to fund ongoing value creation and support research that will lead to future value.

Value Creation

R&D, product design, service quality, customer experience — these create value for the customer

Value Capture

Pricing is the mechanism by which the firm recovers a share of the value it creates — not an arbitrary markup

Strategic Implication

A firm that prices from cost alone captures value by accident — not by design. Market-based pricing captures value intentionally by anchoring price to what customers will actually pay.
