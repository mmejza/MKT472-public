Skim Pricing: Non-Durable vs. Durable Products
Answer: Market A → Skim · Market B → Penetration
Non-Durable Products
e.g., toothpaste, consumables, services
Set price high — keep it there
Price-insensitive segment repurchases continuously at the high price. No durable resale market competes at lower prices.
Durable Products
e.g., televisions, electronics, appliances
Set price high → drop over time
Sequential skim: capture each price-insensitive segment in turn. Price must fall before a used-goods market undercuts the new-product price.
Why Market A Favors Skim

Market A's large price-insensitive Segment 1 will pay a high price. Skim extracts maximum value from this segment before reducing price to access lower segments over time.

Why Market B Favors Penetration

Market B's largest segment is price sensitive. A high price captures only a small, expensive-to-reach fraction of the market. Low penetration price builds volume quickly, lowers unit costs, and creates barriers via scale.
