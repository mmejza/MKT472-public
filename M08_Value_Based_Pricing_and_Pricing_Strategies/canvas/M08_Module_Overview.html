Central Question
What this module asks you to figure out
If you cut price by 10% and volume grows by 25%, does profit go up or down?

Most managers say up. The math says that answer is often wrong. This module trains you to evaluate pricing decisions through customer value, contribution margin, and break-even logic before you move price, not after you lose margin.

You will use three analytical tools - value-in-use, performance value scoring, and break-even pricing analysis - to make a recommendation that is financially defensible and strategically coherent.

!
Core Trap

A 10% price cut with a 25% volume increase can still reduce profit. In this module's benchmark scenario, revenue rises 12.5% while gross profit falls 16.7% because the margin loss is larger than the volume gain.

Two Traps to Watch For

These are the two errors that most often collapse pricing performance in the case analysis and in real organizations.

Starting with internal cost instead of customer value

Cost-based pricing feels safe but often ignores willingness-to-pay and competitive alternatives. It can leave money on the table in high-value segments and overprice low-value segments.

Where to catch it: Chapter 8 value-in-use and perceived-value sections, App 1 output, and App 2 segment scoring.

Treating volume growth as proof a price cut worked

Revenue can rise while contribution declines. Break-even percentage and elasticity checks must be run before recommending a price move.

Where to catch it: Break-even formula sections, App 3 scenarios, and Vantara Case Part 3 diagnostics.
