Market Segmentation
Group customers by similar needs — then identify the characteristics that make segments actionable
The process of grouping customers with similar needs into distinct segments, then identifying the demographics, lifestyles, and usage behaviors that distinguish one group from another — making the segmentation actionable.
Homogeneous Within
Customers inside a segment share similar needs and respond similarly to the same marketing program.
Heterogeneous Between
Customers across segments have meaningfully different needs and respond differently to different programs.
Six Criteria for Effective Segments
Identifiable
Substantial
Accessible
Stable
Differentiable
Actionable
Why It Matters

90% of new product failures trace to poor market segmentation — products designed without a clear segment target fail to resonate with any buyer group.
