Segment Attractiveness: Three-Factor Framework
Step 3 of the 7-step process — score each dimension before committing resources
Market Demand
Market Size — total segment volume in units or dollars
Growth Rate — annual % change in demand
Market Potential — headroom remaining before saturation
Competitive Intensity
Number of Competitors — how many firms are already serving this segment
Ease of Entry — barriers protecting current competitors
Number of Substitutes — alternative solutions customers can choose
Market Access
Customer Familiarity — does the firm know this customer type?
Channel Access — can the firm reach this segment cost-effectively?
Company Fit — do firm capabilities align with segment requirements?
Make-or-Break Factor

Market Access is the veto factor — a highly attractive segment is unreachable without channel access and capability fit. A large, fast-growing segment with 44 competitors can score lower than a small, high-growth segment with 3 competitors. Sub-factors: Ch. 5 Fig. 5-10.
