James Hardie: Before and After Segmentation
Segmentation strategy unlocks price points and margins unavailable in a mass-market approach
Strategic Lever Before — Mass Market After — Two-Segment Strategy
Product Standard fiber cement planks; one product line Quality segment: HardieTrim + ColorPlus pre-finished
Price segment: standard planks
Price Moderate; constrained by price-sensitive buyers in market Quality: 2–3× price premium
Price: standard market rate
Target Customer All fiber cement buyers — mixed quality and price orientation Quality (30%): custom/spec builders
Price (70%): tract/multi-family builders
Gross Margin Blended margin; pulled down by price-sensitive volume Quality segment margin substantially higher; price segment sustains volume
Revenue Growth Growth limited by commodity pricing pressure Quality segment adds premium revenue stream; total growth accelerates
Key Insight

Segmentation allows price discrimination by need — not by demographics. The 30% quality segment pays 2–3× more because the product genuinely addresses their need (installed appearance, job-site efficiency). A single mass-market price would sacrifice either the premium or the volume.
