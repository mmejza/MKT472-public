Targeting Trap: Highest NMC ≠ Best Allocation
NMC measures total return — Marketing ROI measures return per dollar of spend
✗ Wrong Decision Rule
"Target the segment with the highest NMC."

NMC = absolute dollars of marketing profit.

A segment with $29M NMC requires $13.6M in M&S spend → ROI = 214%

This looks like the winner — but only if you ignore what it costs to get there.
✓ Correct Decision Rule
"Target the segment with the highest Marketing ROI."

Marketing ROI = NMC ÷ M&S Expense × 100

A segment with $27M NMC requires $12.2M in M&S spend → ROI = 222%

This segment earns more per dollar invested — the correct priority for a resource-constrained firm.
Rule for Resource-Constrained Firms

When marketing budgets are finite, allocate to segments by Marketing ROI rank, not NMC rank. Higher NMC at lower ROI means you are paying more per dollar of return — a misallocation.
