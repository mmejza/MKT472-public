Forces That Shape Consumer Market Needs
Three forces explain why customers in the same demographic may have entirely different needs
Demographic Influences
Age, income, occupation, education, marital status — changes in life situation shift product needs
Example: College student → management trainee → parent = three different car requirements
Lifestyle Influences
Values, attitudes, interests, activities — two demographically identical consumers may need completely different products
Example: Environmental values → hybrid vehicle; performance values → sports car
Usage Behaviors
How the product is used, when, how much — usage context shapes specification requirements
Example: Family car vs. graduation gift vs. business vehicle — same buyer, three different needs
B2B Parallel — Gate 1
B2B markets have parallel forces: firm demographics, company culture, and usage behaviors (Ch. 5, Fig. 5-6). Growth entrepreneurs vs. cost sustainers is the key B2B segment contrast.
