Attractiveness Reveal: Alpha Wins Despite Being Smaller
Size is one of nine factors — competitive intensity often overrides market demand
Segment Alpha — More Attractive
Market Demand: Small but growing at 28%/yr with high remaining potential — market expanding faster than competitors can fill it

Competitive Intensity: 3 competitors, high entry barriers, few substitutes — margins protected

Market Access: Strong channel relationships already in place
Segment Beta — Less Attractive
Market Demand: Large but growing at only 1%/yr with low remaining potential — near saturation

Competitive Intensity: 44 competitors + low entry barriers = margins continuously competed away as new entrants arrive

Market Access: Weak channel position
Key Insight

44 competitors + low barriers is a structural trap: any margin earned signals entry to more competitors, driving margins back down. Competitive intensity is frequently the decisive factor — it converts large demand into commoditized, low-profit volume.
