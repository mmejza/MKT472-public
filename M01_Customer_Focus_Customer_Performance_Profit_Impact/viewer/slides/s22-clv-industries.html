CLV Varies Across Industries
Online customer NPV over 3 years · 10% annual discount rate
Industry Acq. Cost Year 1 Profit Year 2 Profit Year 3 Profit 3-Year CLV (NPV)
Electronics (online) −$50 $10 $12 $11 −$17.25
Groceries (online) −$90 $15 $60 $57 +$25.77
Apparel (online) −$50 $15 $60 $74 +$80.79
Electronics — Negative CLV
Acquisition cost ($50) exceeds 3-year discounted profits. The customer never fully pays back the cost of acquiring them — negative CLV over the measured horizon.
Groceries — Late Recovery
Highest acquisition cost ($90) but high sustained repeat purchases recover it by year 2. Positive CLV requires customer retention through the payback period.
Apparel — Highest CLV
Same acquisition cost as electronics but a growing annual profit trajectory produces the highest CLV. Profit growth matters as much as profit level.
CLV as a Cross-Industry Strategy Tool

Industry CLV varies not just by profitability per transaction, but by the shape of profit over time. A firm that calculates CLV knows exactly how long it must retain a customer to recover acquisition cost — and can target marketing investment accordingly. Electronics online firms must either cut AC dramatically or find ways to grow year-2 and year-3 repurchase rates.
