Five Customer Loyalty Types
Loyal · Repeat · Captive · New · Unprofitable
Loyal
Highest value customer. Satisfied, committed, refers others, and resists competitive overtures. High CLI and CSI. The firm's most defensible asset.
Repeat
Purchases regularly but has not reached the commitment level of a Loyal customer. Valuable but not yet fully defensible — competitor offers could attract this customer.
Captive
Retained but not loyal. Stays because of switching costs or lack of alternatives — would switch immediately if a viable option appeared. High retention rate, low CLI. See s10 for the Nevada Power example.
New
Recently acquired customer whose long-term relationship potential is not yet established. Still forming expectations and evaluating the firm. The most expensive customer to serve relative to revenue generated.
Unprofitable
Generates negative NMC — the cost to serve exceeds revenue contribution. Chapter 1 identifies two subtypes of unprofitable customers with distinct strategic implications. The names, definitions, and recommended responses to each subtype are Chapter 1 content tested on Gate 1.
Gate 1 Alert — Unprofitable Customer Subtypes

Chapter 1 names two specific subtypes of unprofitable customers and explains what distinguishes them from each other. Gate 1 will ask you to identify both names, characterize each, and state the chapter's strategic recommendation for handling each type. These subtypes do not appear on slides.
