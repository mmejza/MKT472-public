Customer Loyalty Index (CLI)
Five behavioral dimensions averaged into a single 0–100 loyalty score
Dim. 1
Overall Satisfaction
How satisfied is the customer overall with the product or service?
Scored 0–100
Dim. 2
Repurchase Intent
How likely is the customer to buy from this firm again?
Scored 0–100
Dim. 3
First Choice
Would the customer choose this firm first if starting fresh?
Scored 0–100
Dim. 4
Word of Mouth
How likely is the customer to recommend the firm to others?
Scored 0–100
Dim. 5
Price Tolerance
Would the customer stay if the price increased modestly?
Scored 0–100
CLI = (Dim. 1 + Dim. 2 + Dim. 3 + Dim. 4 + Dim. 5) ÷ 5
Equal-weighted average of five behavioral dimensions — result is a 0–100 index
CLI vs. CSI

CSI measures how the customer feels (attitudinal). CLI measures what the customer does and intends (behavioral). High CSI with low CLI = satisfied but uncommitted. High CLI = committed customer regardless of occasional dissatisfaction.
