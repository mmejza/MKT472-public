Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)
Present value of average profit per customer over their expected lifetime
CLV = [M × (r ÷ (1 + i − r))] − AC
M
Margin per customer per period (Revenue − Variable Costs)
r
Retention rate — probability customer purchases again next period (0 to 1)
i
Discount rate — cost of capital per period; must match the period of r and M
AC
Acquisition cost — up-front marketing cost to bring the customer in
Period Matching Rule
r, M, and i must all be measured in the same time period. If r is monthly, i must be a monthly rate and M must be monthly margin.
CLV as Maximum AC
When CLV is positive, the bracketed term exceeds AC. Set CLV = 0 and solve for AC to find the maximum justifiable acquisition cost.
Denominator Alert
If r = 1 − i, the denominator is zero and CLV is undefined. If r > 1 − i, verify your inputs — the denominator should always be positive.
