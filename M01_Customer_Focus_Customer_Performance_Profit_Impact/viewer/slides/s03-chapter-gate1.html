Three Components of Market Orientation
Ch. 1 · Opening sections

Zone of Tolerance
Ch. 1 · Satisfaction measurement section
Chapter 1 defines the zone of tolerance and positions it between a customer's minimum acceptable service level and their desired service level. Draw a quick diagram showing all three levels — Gate 1 will ask you to identify or apply this concept from the chapter alone.

Forward-Looking vs. Backward-Looking Metrics
Ch. 1 · Customer metrics section
Chapter 1 classifies performance metrics as forward-looking or backward-looking and explains the strategic implication of each. Make a two-column list: customer satisfaction, retention, and loyalty on one side; sales revenue and market share on the other. Write the chapter's argument for why one category is more strategically valuable.
4
Loyalty Type Subtypes: Misfits & Spinners
Ch. 1 · Loyalty classification section
Chapter 1 identifies two subtypes of unprofitable customers. Gate 1 tests the names, what distinguishes them, and the strategic recommendation for handling each. Know all five loyalty type names as context — then focus on what makes misfits and spinners different from each other.
5
The MBNA Retention Leverage Result
Ch. 1 · pp. 18–19
Chapter 1 presents a real case in which a small improvement in customer retention produced a disproportionately large increase in profits and a dramatic shift in competitive ranking. Record the three specific outcomes cited and write the chapter's explanation of the mechanism. Gate 1 asks about the mechanism, not just the statistics.
