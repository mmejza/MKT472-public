Retention ≠ Loyalty
Related concepts — but meaningfully different in strategic implication
+
Satisfaction → Retention
Higher customer satisfaction produces higher retention rates. A satisfied customer has no reason to leave and will generally continue purchasing — satisfying customers is necessary for retention.
+
Retention → Loyalty
Higher retention over time supports the development of genuine loyalty. Long-term customers are more likely to refer others, tolerate minor service failures, and resist competitor overtures.
≠
Retention ≠ Loyalty
A customer can be retained without being loyal. Captive customers continue purchasing because they lack alternatives or face high switching costs — not because they choose the brand. They would switch immediately if a viable alternative appeared.
Strategic Implication

Retention metrics can mislead. A firm reporting 90% retention may have 30% of those customers one competitor entry away from defecting. The Customer Loyalty Index (next slide) provides a more complete picture of the quality of the customer relationship.
