The Vicious Circle: No Customer Focus
Each step in the chain makes the next worse — the cycle is self-reinforcing
Step 1
Internal Focus
Strategy driven by internal cost targets and short-term financial goals — not customer needs.
Step 2
Product Misalignment
Products and services fail to meet what customers actually value — satisfaction declines.
Step 3
Customer Defection
Dissatisfied customers leave — especially those with the most value to the firm.
Step 4
Revenue & Margin Pressure
Declining customer base shrinks revenue — management responds with cost cuts to protect margins.
Step 5
Further Underservice → Cycle Repeats
Cost cuts reduce service quality further — the cycle accelerates and becomes harder to break.
Why Market Orientation Breaks the Cycle

Market orientation reverses Step 1 — the firm organizes strategy around customer needs first. When satisfaction rises, defection falls, revenue grows, and investment in quality becomes self-funding rather than a cost drain.
