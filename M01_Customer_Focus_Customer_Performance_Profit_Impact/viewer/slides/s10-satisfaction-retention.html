Customer Satisfaction & Retention
Satisfaction is positively related to retention — but moderating factors temper the link
The Base Relationship
Higher satisfaction produces higher retention. This relationship is directionally consistent across industries and customer types — it is the foundation of LO3 and LO4.
Factors That Moderate the Link
Competitive alternatives: When few alternatives exist, dissatisfied customers stay — not because they are satisfied but because they have nowhere to go.
Switching costs: High switching costs (financial, psychological, or procedural) can trap customers even at low satisfaction levels.
Category involvement: Low-involvement categories produce habitual repurchase regardless of satisfaction — customers don't actively evaluate the relationship.
Example — Captive Market
Nevada Power and Southwest Gas are regulated utilities serving defined geographic markets. Customers cannot switch providers. Retention remains high even when satisfaction is low — not because the firm earned loyalty, but because the customer has no choice.
High retention ≠ high satisfaction in captive markets. Moderating factors must always be assessed before drawing strategic conclusions from retention data alone.
