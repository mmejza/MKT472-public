Retention & Customer Life Expectancy
Two directions — convert between retention rate and expected lifetime
Given Retention Rate → Find Lifetime
N = 1 ÷ (1 − r)
N = average number of purchase periods
r = retention rate (decimal)
Given Lifetime → Find Retention Rate
r = 1 − (1 ÷ N)
r = retention rate (decimal)
N = average customer lifetime in periods
Example — Paul Mitchell Shampoo
Average customer buys 5 times (N = 5)
r = 1 − (1 ÷ 5) = 1 − 0.20 = 80% retention rate

Verify: N = 1 ÷ (1 − 0.80) = 1 ÷ 0.20 = 5 periods ✓
Gate 2 Practice
Q: r = 85% → N = ?
N = 1 ÷ (1 − 0.85) = 1 ÷ 0.15 = 6.67 periods

Q: N = 8.33 → r = ?
r = 1 − (1 ÷ 8.33) = 1 − 0.12 = 88%
Why This Formula Matters for CLV

The N formula connects retention strategy directly to customer lifetime. A 5-point retention increase (80%→85%) extends average lifetime from 5.0 to 6.67 periods — a 33% increase in expected revenue stream. This multiplicative effect is why small retention improvements produce large CLV gains.
