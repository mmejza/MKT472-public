Customer Satisfaction Drives Profitability
The relationship is non-linear — Very Satisfied customers are disproportionately profitable
The Non-Linear Relationship
Higher satisfaction produces higher average profit per customer — but the gains are not evenly distributed across tiers. Moving from "Satisfied" to "Very Satisfied" produces a much larger profit jump than any other tier transition.
Why It Is Non-Linear
Very Satisfied customers buy more, repurchase at higher rates, generate word-of-mouth referrals, and tolerate modest price increases. These compounding effects create profit far above what revenue alone suggests.
