CLV Period-by-Period: Attempt First
Credit card customer — calculate CLV using both methods before the next slide
Given: Credit card customer · Margin per year = $61 · Retention rate = 80% per year · Discount rate = 10% per year · Acquisition cost = $51
Method 1 — Formula
CLV = [M × (r ÷ (1 + i − r))] − AC

CLV = [$61 × (0.80 ÷ (1 + 0.10 − 0.80))] − $51

CLV = [$61 × (0.80 ÷ ?)] − $51 = ?
Method 2 — Period-by-Period PV
Year 1: P(stay) = 1.0 → Profit = $61 → PV = $61 ÷ 1.10 = ?
Year 2: P(stay) = 0.80 → Profit = $48.80 → PV = ?
Year 3: P(stay) = 0.64 → Profit = $39.04 → PV = ?
Sum to ∞ − AC = CLV = ?
Period-by-Period Rule
Expected profit in year t = M × r^(t−1)
PV of year t = Expected profit ÷ (1 + i)^t
CLV = Σ PV of all years − AC
Both Methods Should Agree

The formula method and the period-by-period method produce the same result — the formula is the closed-form solution to the infinite sum. Use this as a check.
