CLV Formula: Solution
ISP example — CLV = $131.33 · Max justifiable acquisition cost = $131.33
VariableValueNote
Price $40.00 / month Monthly period
Variable Costs $1.50 + $1.50 = $3.00 / month Service + loyalty program
Margin (M) $40.00 − $3.00 = $37.00 Monthly contribution per customer
Retention Rate (r) 78.8% = 0.788 Monthly — matches period
Discount Rate (i) 1% = 0.010 Monthly — matches period
Denominator (1 + i − r) 1 + 0.010 − 0.788 = 0.222 Small denominator → large multiplier
Acquisition Cost (AC) $0 Assumed for this problem
CLV $37.00 × (0.788 ÷ 0.222) − $0 = $131.33 Max justifiable AC = $131.33
Interpretation

Each newly acquired customer of this type is worth $131.33 to the firm in present-value terms. The firm should spend no more than $131.33 to acquire a customer like this. Any AC above $131.33 makes CLV negative — the customer never pays back.

Denominator Check

0.222 is small because r = 78.8% and i = 1%. The multiplier r ÷ (1+i−r) = 0.788 ÷ 0.222 = 3.55. This means each $1 of monthly margin is worth about $3.55 in present value — the power of high retention at low discount rates.
