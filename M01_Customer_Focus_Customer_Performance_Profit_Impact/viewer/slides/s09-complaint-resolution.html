Urge Dissatisfied Customers to Complain
Three paths — retention rate depends on which path the customer takes
Path 1 — Satisfied
90%
Retention Rate
A satisfied customer who experiences no problem. High retention is the baseline — this is what the firm is trying to achieve for all customers.
Path 2 — Dissatisfied, Complains
80%
Retention Rate
A dissatisfied customer who voices the complaint. The firm gets a recovery opportunity — effective resolution brings retention close to the satisfied-customer baseline.
Recovery = near-full retention. Every complaint is a recovery opportunity.
Path 3 — Dissatisfied, Silent
17%
Retention Rate
A dissatisfied customer who says nothing and leaves. The firm loses 83% of these customers and never learns why. No recovery is possible — the opportunity is gone.
Silent defection is the worst outcome. The firm must make it easy to complain.
Strategic Implication

The retention gap between Path 2 (80%) and Path 3 (17%) is 63 percentage points. That gap is almost entirely explained by whether the customer got a chance to complain. Firms that suppress complaints lose customers at catastrophic rates — firms that invite complaints recover most of them.
