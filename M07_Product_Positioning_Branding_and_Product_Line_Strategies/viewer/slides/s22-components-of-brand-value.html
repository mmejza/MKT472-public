Components of Brand Value
Four types of value — all generated from the stories a brand accumulates
The Objective Foundation

The product itself (objective features, quality standards, augmentations) is the necessary base — but brand value is created by the meaning layered on top of the product through storytelling and experience.

Reputation Value
Brand stories imply perceived quality of product features — customers infer that the product is better because the brand has a strong reputation, even before direct experience
Symbolic Value
Brand stories allow the product to become a value or identity marker — customers use the brand to signal membership, status, values, or self-concept to others
Relationship Value
Brand stories imply the firm is a long-term partner that attends to customer interests — customers extend trust based on perceived reliability and responsiveness over time
Experiential Value
Brand stories shape the experience of the product — expectations set by the brand alter how the customer perceives, interprets, and remembers the actual consumption experience
