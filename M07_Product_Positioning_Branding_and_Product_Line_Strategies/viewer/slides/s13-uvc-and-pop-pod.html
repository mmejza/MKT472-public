Identifying Unique Attributes: POP vs. POD
Points of parity qualify you to compete — points of differentiation give you a reason to win
Points of Parity (POP)
Attributes that all brands in the category offer
Necessary to be considered in the competitive set
Provide no competitive advantage — but their absence disqualifies
e.g., For a car: engine, safety ratings, warranty
Points of Differentiation (POD)
Attributes that competing brands lack — unique to this brand
Provide a specific, defensible reason to choose this brand
Must be valued by the target segment to create competitive advantage
e.g., For a car: specific performance metric, design exclusivity
Strategic Diagnostic

Map competitor attributes vs. your brand attributes. The overlap zone = POP (cost of entry). Your exclusive zone = POD candidates. A viable positioning requires at least one POD that the target segment values and that competitors cannot immediately replicate.
