Product Mix Decisions: Width, Depth, Consistency
Three dimensions — every product portfolio decision maps to one or more of these
Width
Breadth
How many distinct product lines does the firm offer?
P&G has wide breadth — detergents, diapers, razors, oral care, paper products
Depth
Items per line
How many distinct items exist within each product line?
Apple's iPhone line has deep depth — 15, 15 Pro, 15 Pro Max, 15 Plus, multiple storage tiers
Consistency
Relatedness
How closely related are lines in usage, buyers, or production technology?
High consistency → operational leverage. Low consistency → diversification risk but market reach.
Strategic Tradeoff

Increasing width adds market coverage but reduces consistency. Increasing depth adds segment coverage but adds complexity and cost. Every product mix decision requires an explicit tradeoff across all three dimensions.
