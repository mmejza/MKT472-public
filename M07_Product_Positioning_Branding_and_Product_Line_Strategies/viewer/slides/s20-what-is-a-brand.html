What Is a Brand?
The legal definition is necessary — but not sufficient
Legal Definition

Brand: A name, term, design, symbol, or any other feature that identifies one seller's good or service and distinguishes it from those of other sellers.

Trademark: The legal protection that grants exclusive use of the brand to its owner — enforced through registration and law.

But Is a Brand More Than This?

A trademark is a legal instrument. A brand is a cultural artifact.

A brand creates meaning — through stories, associations, experiences, and relationships — that extends far beyond the physical product and its legal identifier.

The Strategic Gap

Two firms can have identical products and identical trademarks in legal strength — but radically different brand values. The difference is not in the product; it is in the accumulated meaning that the brand carries in the minds of its customers, influencers, and culture.
