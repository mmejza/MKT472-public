Consumer Products Classification
Buying behavior and brand insistence determine product type — and the right marketing strategy
Dimension Convenience Shopping Specialty Unsought
Purchase Frequency Frequent; minimal effort Infrequent; compare brands & stores Infrequent; extensive pre-search Infrequent; some comparison
Brand Insistence Aware; will accept substitutes Certain brands; will accept others One brand only; no substitutes Will accept substitutes
Examples Toothpaste, pasta, candy bars Apparel, cameras, televisions Maserati cars, Coach bags Funeral services, life insurance
Distribution Intensive — everywhere Selective — quality stores Exclusive — controlled channels Direct / push-driven
Strategic Takeaway

Product classification dictates marketing mix decisions. Convenience goods require intensive distribution and low friction. Specialty goods require exclusive distribution and high brand investment — availability matters less than desirability. Misclassifying a product leads to mismatched strategy.
