Product Mix Vocabulary
Three levels of aggregation — Item → Line → Portfolio
Product Item
A specific product or service denoted by a distinct brand name, size, or price point — the most granular unit of the portfolio
e.g., Tide Original Scent, 64 oz liquid — a single SKU
Product Line
A group of product items that are related in terms of usage, buyer segments, or technical characteristics — managed together as a unit
e.g., All Tide laundry detergent variants (pods, liquid, powder, free & gentle)
Product Mix / Portfolio
The complete set of all product lines and items that a company offers for sale — the totality of the firm's market-facing product structure
e.g., P&G's full portfolio: Tide, Pampers, Gillette, Oral-B, Bounty, and 60+ brands across product lines
Why It Matters

Portfolio decisions — which items to add, drop, or reposition — are made at the line and mix level, not the item level. Understanding aggregation levels is prerequisite to any product strategy decision.
