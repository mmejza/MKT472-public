Approaches to Differentiation
Five levers — a company can differentiate on any combination
Product Performance
Reliability, durability, feature set, design quality
Service Quality
Responsiveness, reliability, assurance, empathy, tangibles
Brand Reputation
Perceived quality, trust, heritage, symbolic identity
Price
Relative price position vs. competitors in the competitive set
Ownership Costs
Total cost of owning, operating, and disposing of the product over its life
The Positioning Planning Framework

For each of the five levers, a firm assesses its current value vs. competitors and its target future value. The gap between current and future defines the repositioning investment. Slides 18–19 apply this framework to a case.

Nike SHOX Example

Product performance differentiation: the SHOX column technology provided a specific, measurable, and visible performance attribute — cushioning and spring return — that could be demonstrated and experienced. It shifted shoe purchasing from brand loyalty to technical performance evaluation in target segment.
