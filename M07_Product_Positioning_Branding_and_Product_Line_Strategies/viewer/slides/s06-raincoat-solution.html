Customer Value Hierarchy: Raincoat — Solution
Each level defines the competitive minimum — and the differentiation opportunity above it
LevelLabelRaincoat ExampleCompetitive Meaning
5Potential Product Smart coat — temperature-adaptive, GPS-enabled, app-connected Future competitive frontier
4Augmented Product Extra-light, high-durability material; free accessories POD — differentiates today
3Expected Product Standard cut, hood, multiple color options, basic closures POP — must have to compete
2Basic Product Waterproof material, common sizes Functional minimum
1Core Benefit Protection from weather Customer's real purchase motive
Strategic Takeaway

Level 4 advantages erode as competitors imitate. Today's POD becomes tomorrow's POP — which forces repositioning. The manager's job is to continuously invest in Level 4 before competitors reach parity.
