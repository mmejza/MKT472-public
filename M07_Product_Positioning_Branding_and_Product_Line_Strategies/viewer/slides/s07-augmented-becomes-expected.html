Augmented Becomes Expected
POD erodes into POP as competitors imitate — forcing continuous repositioning
The Mechanism

A firm introduces an augmented attribute (POD). Competitors observe the attribute and imitate it. The attribute becomes industry-standard. It migrates from Level 4 → Level 3 — from differentiator to minimum requirement.

The Consequence

The firm loses its differentiation without losing the cost of producing the formerly-augmented attribute. Margin erodes. Repositioning becomes necessary — not optional.

Competitive Erosion Pattern
Year 1
Firm launches augmented feature → POD → premium pricing justified
Year 3
Competitors imitate → attribute becomes common → price pressure begins
Year 5
Feature is now POP — expected by all buyers — no longer a reason to buy
Chapter Analog — Gate 1

Samsung's repositioning (1997–2005) is the textbook case. ROA moved from −3.5% to +9.4% through deliberate repositioning across all five value dimensions. (pp. 233–234, Fig 7-5)
