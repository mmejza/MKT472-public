Branding Policies
Three approaches — each reflects a different tradeoff between brand leverage and risk
Multi-Product Branding
Definition
One brand name used for all products in a product class — brand equity built centrally, applied broadly
e.g., Sony (all electronics), Apple (all devices)
Tradeoff
+ Leverages existing equity · Lower launch cost
− One product failure harms entire brand
Multi-Brand Policy
Definition
Each product or product line is given a distinct name — segments served independently without brand overlap
e.g., P&G: Tide, Cheer, Ariel (separate laundry brands)
Tradeoff
+ Isolates brand risk · Targets distinct segments
− High investment per brand; resources fragmented
Private Branding
Definition
Company supplies a reseller with a product bearing a brand name chosen by the reseller — manufacturer's brand does not appear
e.g., Kirkland (Costco), store-brand cereals
Tradeoff
+ Volume and shelf access; lower marketing cost
− No brand equity built; reseller controls the brand
