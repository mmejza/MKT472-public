Features, Functions, and Benefits
A feature allows a function that provides a benefit — the brand ladder
Core Principle

A feature enables a function (how the product works) that delivers a benefit (the value the customer receives). Customers buy benefits — not features.

Example: Shampoo

Feature: Protein compounds in formula → Function: Coat and strengthen hair shaft → Benefit: Increased visual appeal and perceived health

Features
Ingredients · Components · Materials · Esthetic traits · Services · Price point
Functions
Propels vehicle · Carries liquids · Hides blemishes · Untangles hair · Dissolves rust · Sweetens taste
Benefits
Saves time or effort · Sensory enjoyment · Economic gains · Cognitive experiences · Reduces anxiety
Differentiation Implication

Positioning at the feature level is easily imitated. Positioning at the benefit level is harder to copy — because it requires the competitor to match not just the feature but the entire functional chain and the emotional resonance it creates.
