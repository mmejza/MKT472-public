Brand Culture
Brand meaning is built from shared stories — the firm controls only one source
Brand Culture Defined

The shared stories, images, and associations that collectively define what a brand means — beyond its product attributes and legal identity.

Company Brand Stories
Advertising, product design, packaging, communications — the intended brand narrative that the firm authors and controls
Customers' Brand Stories
Reviews, word-of-mouth, social posts, user-generated content — authentic customer experiences that build or erode meaning independent of the firm
Influencers' Brand Stories
Creator endorsements, media coverage, expert reviews — third-party narratives that carry credibility the company cannot self-generate
Popular Culture Brand Stories
Film placements, cultural moments, celebrity associations — brand meaning embedded in broader cultural narratives without direct firm involvement
Strategic Implication

A firm that only manages its own brand stories manages 25% of its brand meaning — at best. Brand management requires monitoring and responding to all four story streams, not just producing advertising.
