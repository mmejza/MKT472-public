Strategy Brand / Product Class Examples Gate 2 Note
Line Extension
Existing brand, existing class		 Adds variety within a known category Dannon Fruit on the Bottom; iPhone 15 Pro Easy to identify — same brand, same aisle
Brand Extension
Existing brand, new class		 Leverages brand into a different market Honda outboard engines; Bic pantyhose Key test: is it a genuinely new product category?
Fighter / Flanker
New brand, existing class		 Defensive new brand in a market the firm already serves Luv Diapers (flanks Pampers); Cricket Wireless (AT&T flanker) Trap: Often confused with Brand Extension — the distinction is existing vs. new product class
New Brand
New brand, new class		 Entirely new market entry with no brand leverage Aquafina (Pepsi enters bottled water); Dasani (Coca-Cola enters bottled water) Highest investment; no equity to borrow
The Fighter/Flanker Distinction — Know Cold

A fighter brand is created in the same product class the parent already serves — to protect against a specific competitive threat at a lower price point. It is NOT a brand extension into a new category. Luv flanks Pampers in diapers. Cricket flanks AT&T in wireless. Same class; new brand.
