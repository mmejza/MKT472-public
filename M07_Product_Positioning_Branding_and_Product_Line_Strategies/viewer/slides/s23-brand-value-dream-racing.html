Brand Value in Action: Dream Racing
One brand — four types of value — four customer statements
Reputation Value
"I drove a well-tuned, fast accelerating Ferrari. A professional race driver showed me how to navigate the track."
→ Brand stories create an inference of product and service quality before the experience occurs
Relationship Value
"When I had to cancel at the last minute, Dream Racing fully refunded me — no questions asked."
→ Brand behavior signals a long-term partnership orientation — the firm attends to customer interests even at a cost to itself
Experiential Value
"I confronted and conquered my fear at Dream Racing."
→ Brand narrative shaped how the customer interpreted the experience — fear became a feature, not a deterrent
Symbolic Value
"What do I do for fun? I'm a Dream Racing person."
→ Brand became an identity marker — the customer uses it to define and signal who they are to others
Strategic Reveal

Dream Racing sells a commodity experience (track driving). Its competitive advantage is entirely in brand value — no product attributes differentiate it from competitors. All four value types are built through story, not product.
