How each module works

Step 1

Read the chapter. All modules require the textbook first — slides and video are supplements, not replacements.

Step 2

Pass Gate 1 (chapter-only questions) to unlock the interactive slide viewer.

Step 3

Work through the HTML slide viewer. Embedded check questions pause progress at key moments.

Step 4

Pass Gate 2 (chapter + slides synthesis) to unlock the lecture video.

Step 5

Watch the lecture video (~30 min), complete apps and worksheets, then submit the case analysis.

Part I — Market Orientation & Performance Metrics  (M01–M02)

M01 · Built Market Orientation & Customer Lifetime Value Ch. 1 ▶

Establishes that firm profitability depends on how well a company satisfies and retains customers over time. The central challenge — the distributions-versus-averages problem — leads students to a counterintuitive insight: eliminating dissatisfied customers often reduces average profit rather than raising it.

  • LO1Trace the causal chain from insufficient customer focus through declining satisfaction to diminished shareholder value.
  • LO2Calculate and interpret a weighted-average Customer Satisfaction Index (CSI).
  • LO3Assess the financial case for investing in a complaint-resolution program.
  • LO4Distinguish customer retention from loyalty; classify customers; calculate a Customer Loyalty Index.
  • LO5Calculate customer life expectancy from a retention rate using N = 1 ÷ (1 − r).
  • LO6Compute CLV using the closed-form formula and the period-by-period present value method.
M02 · Built Marketing Metrics & Marketing Profitability Ch. 2 ▶

Builds the financial vocabulary of marketing strategy. The central counterintuitive result: dropping a product line with negative net profit often reduces total company profit — a direct consequence of how net marketing contribution (NMC) isolates marketing's contribution from shared overhead.

  • LO1Distinguish financial (internal) performance metrics from marketing (external) performance metrics.
  • LO2Classify the four categories of marketing metrics and explain the forward/backward-looking distinction.
  • LO3Calculate net marketing contribution (NMC) and explain how it differs from net profit.
  • LO4Calculate marketing ROS and marketing ROI; benchmark against Fortune 500 averages.
  • LO5Use NMC decomposition to plan and evaluate alternative marketing strategies.
  • LO6Apply the market size growth formula and interpret NMC product/customer line management insights.

Part II — Market Analysis  (M03–M05)

M03 · Built Market Potential, Market Demand & Market Share Ch. 3 ▶

Establishes the measurement vocabulary for sizing and tracking markets — how to calculate market potential, measure development stage, apply the product life cycle, and diagnose a company's share position relative to its potential.

  • LO1Distinguish generic, product category, and product form markets; construct examples of each.
  • LO2Define market potential and apply the formula to calculate unit and dollar potential.
  • LO3Calculate and interpret the Market Development Index (MDI).
  • LO4Identify the seven forces that prevent markets from reaching full potential and the adoption forces.
  • LO5Describe the four PLC stages and explain how demand, profit, pricing, and strategy differ across them.
  • LO6Calculate market share; construct and interpret a market share performance tree and MSI.
  • LO7Calculate SDI; use the MDI × SDI growth opportunity portfolio to classify growth strategies.
M04 · Built Customer Value & the Customer Experience Ch. 4 ▶

Moves from market-level metrics to the individual customer — how companies quantify the value their products deliver across four distinct dimensions and use customer experience insights to drive product and service improvements.

  • LO1Define economic value and explain how lifecycle costs determine it (EV = Competitor LC Cost − Our LC Cost).
  • LO2Describe three ways a business can create more economic value.
  • LO3Explain why willingness-to-pay cannot be assumed from economic value alone.
  • LO4Calculate relative performance value using the fair price line and price-performance ratio.
  • LO5Calculate perceived value using importance-performance analysis and interpret the value map.
  • LO6Explain conjoint analysis (CVI) and emotional value / brand personality.
